About The Modern Frame

Celebrating contemporary art and fostering cultural dialogue since 1995

Our History

Founded in 1995 by art enthusiasts Margaret Collins and Robert Kim, The Modern Frame began as a small exhibition space in the heart of the Creative District. What started as a passion project has evolved into one of the city's most respected contemporary art venues.

Our mission has always been to bridge the gap between emerging artists and established collectors, providing a platform where innovative artistic expression can flourish. Over nearly three decades, we have showcased over 300 exhibitions and supported more than 150 artists in their creative journeys.

The gallery relocated to its current 5,000 square foot space in 2010, featuring state-of-the-art lighting systems and climate-controlled environments designed to showcase artwork in optimal conditions.

Notable Artists

We are proud to have worked with these exceptional contemporary artists:

  • Marcus Chen - Sculptor working with sustainable materials and urban themes
  • Sarah Mitchell - Contemporary painter exploring environmental consciousness
  • David Park - Installation artist focusing on spatial relationships
  • Luna Vasquez - Mixed media artist blending digital and traditional techniques
  • James Wong - Minimalist sculptor and conceptual artist
  • Aisha Okafor - Textile artist celebrating cultural heritage
  • Alex Thompson - Documentary photographer and visual storyteller
